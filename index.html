Haruka Kusakabe | 日下部　晴香
I am a researcher working on galaxy formation and evolution, in terms of the cosmic baryon cycle, using observational data primarily from VLT/MUSE, HST, and MeerKAT to study galaxies from the local to distant Universe in the Department of General Systems Studies, Graduate School of Arts and Sciences, The University of Tokyo. I obtained my PhD from the Department of Astronomy at the University of Tokyo in 2019 under the supervision of Kazuhiro Shimasaku. Following that, I worked on the observations of the circumgalactic medium and cosmic reionization with Anne Verhamme at the University of Geneva from 2019 to 2023. From April 2023 to March 2025, I was a Japan Society for the Promotion of Science (JSPS) Postdoctoral Fellow at the Division of Science, National Astronomical Observatory of Japan (in Masami Ouchi's group).
